Sous Vide Steak

As the title says this is a very simple sous vide steak recepie. Sous Vide is a method of cooking vacuum sealed food, specially meats and fish, in a warm water bath for long period of time anywhere from 1 to 36hrs depending of the food being cooked. This method makes cooking a tender steak to the desired temperature much easier and consistant. Its main downsisde is the amount of time it takes to cook the steak and the extra equipment requirement. You will need a sous vide capable machine, there are many out there and prices can range from as low as $59.99 to $500+. I recomend the Anova precision or the Ninja foodi if you are looking an air-fryer combo. Last but not least to cook the perfect sous vide steak you will also need a grill, cast iron skillet, air-fryer or oven.

Ingredients:

  • Butter
  • Black Pepper
  • Steak of choice
  • 2 Cloves of garlic per steak
  • Steak seasoning of choice or salt

Steps:

  1. Bring steaks to room temperature then apply seasoning and black pepper on both sides. Let the seasoning absorb for 30-60 minutes.

  2. Place steak, two crushed cloves of garlic and a bit of butter in zip-lock bag or use a vacuum sealer.

  3. Set the sous vide cooker to your desired temperature:

    Note: I suggest subtracting 10°F and achieving the final temperature when searing.

    • 125° - 130°F for Medium Rare
    • 135° - 140°F for Medium
    • 145° - 150°F for Well Done

  4. To avoid overcooking wiat for water to reach temperature and submerge steak in water ensuring it is completely submerged for even cooking. If using zip-lock submerge steak in water wtiht the zip-lock open and allow the water to pushe the air out then seal the zip-lock. If unable to completely submerge turn steak half way through the cooking process.

  5. Cook for 1 to 3hrs. Note: do not cook for longer than 3hrs as the fibers in the meat will start to break down and the steak will become mushy. For best results cook 1 to 2hrs. The thickness of the cut will determine where in the range you fall with the rule of thumb being: the thicker the cut the longer the time.

  6. Remove steak from and pad dry. The last step is to get a nice sear on the steak:

    Note: For best sear a grill or cast iron skillet is best. Oven or air-fryer will tend
    to over cook the steak you can use a pan in liu of a cast iron skillet.

    • Grill: Preheat to 450°F brush steaks with melted butter and cook on both side for ~1min on each side or until desired sear level.
    • Cast Iron Skillet: Set stove to Medium-high brush steak with melted butter and sear ~1min on each side or until esried sear level.
      Note: wait for the skillet to get hot or you'll over cook your steaks.
    • Air-Fryer: Preheat Air-Fryer in broil mode or to 450°F brush steaks with melted butter and airr fry for ~3mins or until sear but be careful not to overcook your steak always follow the high fast rule better a subpar sear than an overcooked steak.
    • Oven: Preheat oven on broil. Brush streaks with butter and place in oven safe pan or cast iron skillet. Do Not use a non-stick pan as most of them are not rated tp high heat and the coatng will degrade and contaminate your food. Place in oven and broil for ~2-3mins. Be careful not to overcook so alwasy follow the high and fast rule better a subpar sear than an overcooked steak.
