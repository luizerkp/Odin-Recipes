Dominican 7 meats Sancocho

Sancocho is one the most cherished dsihes in the Dominican Republic, whenever there is a gathering, you are very likely to see sancocho being served. This dish is a staple at weddings, birthdays, parties, and even funerals. This dish consists of various pieces of meat, vegies and tubers cooked slowly in a broth. It is traditionally served with a side of white rice and a slice of avocado.

Ingredients:

  • 2lbs beef for stew preferably w/ bones
  • 1lb chopped goat/lamb meat
  • 1lb pork sausage or longaniza
  • 1lb pork any cut preferably w/ bones
  • 1.5lbs chicken preferably w/ bones
  • 2lbs pork ribs
  • 1lb smoked ham bones
  • 4 Limes
  • 2tbsp of crushed Garlic
  • 1 large onion
  • 2tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 1tsp cilantro
  • .5tsp oregano
  • 1.5tsp salt
  • 1.5tsp black pepper
  • .5tsp chicken bouillon
  • 2 corn on the cob
  • 3 unripe plantain
  • .5lb yam
  • .5lb malanga
  • .5lb cassava
  • .5lb west indian pumpkin or kabocha squash
  • 10.5oz King oyster mushroom
  • .5lb carrots
  • Chicken or beef stock

Steps:

  1. In separate bowls season the beef, goat/lamb, pork and chicken with the juice of 1-lime, 1/4-tsp of salt, and 1/4-tsp of black pepper. Mariate for at least 30mins but 1hr-1.5hrs will produce best results.
  2. While th meat marinates peel and cube the cassava, yam, malanga, and pumpkin/squash. Peel and slice plantains in ~1/8th inch slices but save half a plantain for later. Grate the left over plantain into a bowl for later. Cut mushroom and carrots into medium sized pieces. Chop cilantro and onion. Cut corn into 1/2 inch slices.
  3. After meat finishes marinating, in large pot heat oil on medium-high heat. Add cilantro, garlic, onion, oregano, remaining salt, and remaining pepper. Cook until fragrant (~2mins). Next you will want to add the meats in sequence:
    Note: reduce heat to medium after adding the first meat
    • 1st add beef, cook for 10mins
    • 2nd add goat/lamb, cook for 10mins
    • 3rd add pork and pork ribs, cook for 10mins
    • 4th add chicken, cook for 5mins
  4. Add the cassava, yam, malanga, pumpkin/squash and sliced plantain. Add enough water or stock to cover the top of the vegetables and bring heat to medium-high. Add bouillon and the juice of the remaining lime. After water/stock starts boiling lower heat to low and bring to a simmer. Add smoked bones, sussage, grated plantain and mushroom. Let it simmer until everything is completely cooked adding water/stock as neccesaary. Add corn and carrots, cook for ~10mins or until corn is completely cooked. Taste broth and add salt if needed. The broth should have thickenned by now but if not thick enough cook with open lid until broth thinkens.
